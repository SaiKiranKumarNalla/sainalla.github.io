3DynaPET

QuestionHow can we create trustworthy ground-truth data to validate imaging systems and future AI models before they influence clinical decisions?
Expand details
ContextAI in medical imaging needs explainable benchmarks, not only black-box performance scores.
ProcessBuilt a physical phantom workflow where uptake, geometry, and acquisition conditions can be controlled.
Methods3D printing, dynamic imaging, MATLAB analysis, quantitative validation, reproducible protocols.
OutputAn AI-ready validation artifact that helps compare algorithms against known physical truth.
#explainable-AI#ground-truth#clinical-validation

CASSOULET / MuPET

QuestionHow can synthetic medical-imaging data be generated with known truth so AI models can be tested, explained, and trusted?
Expand details
ContextClinical datasets are limited, noisy, and often lack precise lesion-level truth for AI evaluation.
ProcessModelled controlled lesion cohorts using simulation and physics-inspired transport behaviour.
MethodsMonte Carlo logic, MATLAB/Python workflows, synthetic data generation, imaging physics.
OutputA framework direction for explainable AI benchmarking with interpretable synthetic ground truth.
#explainable-AI#synthetic-data#model-validation
