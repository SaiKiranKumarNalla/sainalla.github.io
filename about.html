Who I Am

I'm Sai — a researcher, builder, and someone who's genuinely happiest when working at the edge of what they understand. I grew up in Hyderabad, studied mechanical engineering, and then moved to France on a scholarship to pursue biomedical engineering at École Polytechnique. Now I'm finishing a PhD in medical imaging at the IUCT Oncopole in Toulouse.

I'm the kind of person who reads VC fund reports for fun, stays up late debugging MATLAB code, and still finds time to get lost in Assassin's Creed or Ghost of Tsushima. I think in systems — whether that's the physics of a PET scanner, the structure of a startup pitch, or the flow of a well-designed user interface.

I'm not someone who fits neatly into one box. I've worked in hospitals, startups, and research labs. I've built 3D-printed phantoms, written predictive models for cancer treatment devices, and drafted IP strategy documents. I'm equally comfortable presenting to surgeons as I am to investors.

The Journey

2016 — 2020
始
It started with a curiosity about how things move — fluids, forces, pressure waves. At Mahindra University, I studied mechanical engineering but quickly drifted toward biomechanics. I ended up simulating blood flow and gas embolism in arteries, which became my first journal publication. I also led an autonomous underwater vehicle team, which taught me more about leadership than any textbook could.
2021 — 2023
修
France changed everything. École Polytechnique threw me into a world of soft matter physics, drug delivery research, and deep-tech startup culture. I worked simultaneously at Manitty (a startup making polymer electrodes), at a hospital shadowing cardiologists and 3D-printing surgical benches, and at Hepta Medical — where I built predictive models for a cancer treatment device and translated R&D into investor-facing demos. That's when I realized I wanted to sit at the intersection of research and strategy.
2023 — Now
博
My PhD is about making PET/CT imaging more honest — building simulation tools and physical phantoms that test whether scanners and algorithms actually measure what they claim to. I work with GE Healthcare, collaborate across institutions, and manage the full lifecycle of research: from MATLAB code to 3D printing to clinical validation to writing papers. I've published 6 papers, including preprints under review for peer-reviewed journals. Defense is scheduled for September 2026.
What's Next
先
I'm drawn to where deep-tech research meets strategic decision-making — healthcare ventures, life sciences consulting, biotech VC, or any role where technical depth meets business thinking. I completed the McKinsey Forward Program, built a VC education framework from scratch, and I'm actively exploring opportunities in Paris, London, Amsterdam, and Berlin.

How I Think

橋
The Bridge-Builder
I connect worlds that don't usually talk to each other — physics and oncology, code and clinic, research and commercial strategy. I find the most interesting problems live in the gaps between disciplines.
匠
The Craftsman
I care about doing things properly. Whether it's a MATLAB pipeline, a thesis chapter, or a phantom geometry — I obsess over precision, reproducibility, and getting the details right before moving on.
問
The Questioner
I'm not satisfied with "it works" — I want to know why it works, where it breaks, and what happens at the edges. This drives my research but also makes me a better collaborator and problem-solver.
動
The Builder
I bias toward action. Ideas are interesting, but I want to see the prototype, run the simulation, write the first draft. I learn by making things and iterating — not by waiting for the perfect plan.
