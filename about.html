Five moments

Start

Engineering gave me the base language.

Mechanical engineering taught me structure, systems, and the habit of asking how things actually work.

France

Biomedical engineering changed the scale.

École Polytechnique and France opened the bridge between mechanics, biology, medicine, and research culture.

Imaging

Medical imaging became the core path.

PET/CT, phantoms, simulation, and quantitative validation became the place where my technical interests clicked.

Startup

Translation mattered more than I expected.

Hepta Medical and tech-transfer exposure showed me that research only becomes useful when people can understand and trust it.

Now

The next step is a hybrid one.

I’m aiming for roles where research depth, medtech thinking, product sense, and strategy can sit in the same room.
